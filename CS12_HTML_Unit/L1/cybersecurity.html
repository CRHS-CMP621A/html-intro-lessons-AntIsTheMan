CyberSecurity Attacks In History

1. Stuxnet (2010)

Often called the world's first true "digital weapon," Stuxnet was a hyper-sophisticated computer worm designed for a single, physical purpose: sabotaging Iran’s nuclear program. Kaspersky

The Attack: Unlike typical malware that steals credit cards, Stuxnet targeted Programmable Logic Controllers (PLCs)—the tiny computers that run industrial machinery. It specifically looked for the centrifuges used to enrich uranium. Once inside, it forced them to spin at wildly fluctuating speeds until they literally tore themselves apart.

The "Magic": To hide its tracks, it sent fake "everything is fine" data to the plant’s monitors, so engineers didn't realize anything was wrong until the machines exploded. It was so advanced it used four "zero-day" exploits (unpatched flaws) at once, something almost unheard of even today. Wikipedia

2. WannaCry (2017)

The Attack: It used an exploit called EternalBlue (allegedly stolen from the NSA) to spread automatically from one computer to another without a user ever clicking a link. Once it hit a machine, it encrypted all the files and demanded a $300–$600 ransom in Bitcoin to unlock them.

The Impact: It famously crippled the UK’s National Health Service (NHS), forcing hospitals to cancel surgeries and divert ambulances. The chaos only stopped when a researcher, Marcus Hutchins, accidentally discovered a "kill switch" in the code—a hidden web domain that, once registered, told the malware to stop spreading.
